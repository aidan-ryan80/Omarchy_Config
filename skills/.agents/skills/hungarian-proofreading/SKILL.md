# Hungarian message drafting and proofreading

Draft or proofread Hungarian texts. Two registers: **informal/tegező** (family, friends, group chat) and **formal/magázó** (officials, professionals, strangers). Always include an English translation.

## Output format

1. The **Hungarian text** (ready to send / paste)
2. An **English translation** (for reference)
3. A short note on register / tone if relevant

## Register guide

| Register | When | Addressing | Verb endings |
|---|---|---|---|
| Informal (tegező) | Friends, family, peers, group chat | "Szia/Sziasztok", first name | -sz, -od, -tok, -szátok |
| Formal (magázó) | Officials, lawyers, doctors, strangers | "Tisztelt ...", "Kedves ...", title+Úr/Asszony | -ja, -ta, -ták, -ná |

## Alphabet and diacritics

Hungarian uses Latin script with diacritics. These are **not optional** — they
change meaning:

| Letter | Example | Meaning change if wrong |
|---|---|---|
| a / á | kár (damage) vs kar (arm) | Different word |
| e / é | eger (mouse of) vs éger (alder) | Different word |
| i / í | ír (write) vs ir (tend to) | Different word |
| o / ó | kor (age) vs kór (disease) | Different word |
| ö / ő | ön (self/you) vs őn (on him) | Different word |
| u / ú | üt (hit) vs út (road) | Different word |
| ü / ű | üz (operate) vs űz (chase) | Different word |

Always check: **ő** (long ö, typed as AltGr+Ű on QWERTY) vs **ö** — they are
different letters, not styling.

## Common language issues for English speakers

- **Word order** is topic-comment, not subject-verb-object. The emphasized
  element goes first. "Én szeretlek" (I love YOU) vs "Szeretlek én" (I do love
  you — emphasis on loving).
- **No verb "to have"** — possession uses "-nak/-nek" + "van":
  "Nekem van" = "I have" (lit. "to-me there-is").
- **Definite vs indefinite conjugation** — "Látok egy autót" (I see a car,
  indefinite) vs "Látom az autót" (I see the car, definite). Getting this wrong
  sounds very foreign.
- **Vowel harmony** — suffixes change vowel to match the stem:
  -hoz/-hez/-höz (to), -ban/-ben (in), -val/-vel (with).
- **Plural after numbers** — "5 ház" not "5 házak". Number + singular.
- **No gender** — "ő" = he/she, no distinction.

## Common phrases

### Greetings — informal
- Szia! — Hi (one person)
- Sziasztok! — Hi (multiple)
- Helló / Szevasz — Hello (very casual)
- Cső / Csá — Bye / See ya (very casual)
- Puszi — Kisses (close friends/family)

### Greetings — formal
- Tisztelt ...! — Dear ... (very formal)
- Kedves ...! — Dear ... (standard formal)
- Tisztelt Hivatal! — Dear Office (to an institution)

### Scheduling / calling
- Ráérsz ma este 18:00-kor? — Are you free tonight at 18:00? (informal)
- Ráérnek ma este 18:00-kor? — Are you (pl) free tonight at 18:00?
- Ráér ma este 18:00-kor? — Are you free tonight at 18:00? (formal)
- Szeretnék egy hívást szervezni — I'd like to schedule a call
- ... kapcsán / ügyében — ... regarding ...
- Jó lenne egy gyors egyeztetés — It'd be good to have a quick sync
- Mikor ráztok? — When are you free? (very casual, group)
- Addig is — Until then / In the meantime

### Email
- Csatolva küldöm ... — Attached please find ...
- Mellékelve ... — Enclosed ...
- Válaszát előre is köszönöm — Thank you in advance for your reply (formal)
- Kérem, jelezze — Please let me know (formal)
- Kérlek, jelezzétek — Please let me know (informal, plural)

### Closings
| Situation | Hungarian | English |
|---|---|---|
| Formal | Üdvözlettel | Best regards / Sincerely |
| Semi-formal | Üdv | Cheers / Regards |
| Warm formal | Szívélyes üdvözlettel | Warm regards |
| Family/friends | Puszi / Ölel | Kisses / Hugs |
| Friends | Csá / Szia | Bye |
| Waiting reply | Várom válaszát (formal) / Várom a válaszotokat (informal) | Looking forward |

## Dash style

Do not use em-dashes (—) or en-dashes (-) as sentence punctuation. If a
sentence naturally calls for a dash, rephrase it to flow without one. A comma,
a semicolon, a colon, or a full stop is almost always cleaner. Normal hyphens
joining compound words (e.g. "well-known", "state-of-the-art") are fine; this
rule is about dashes used as punctuation only.

Examples:
- "Judit, Timi — ráztok?" → "Judit, Timi, ráztok?"
- "Egy dolog biztos — nem adom fel" → "Egy dolog biztos: nem adom fel" or
  "Az biztos, hogy nem adom fel."

## General proofreading checklist

1. Correct register (tegező vs magázó)?
2. All names spelled correctly?
3. Diacritics correct (ő/ű, not ö/ü)?
4. Vowel harmony on suffixes (-ban/-ben, -hoz/-hez/-höz)?
5. Definite/indefinite conjugation correct?
6. Date format consistent (Hungarian: YYYY.MM.DD. or YYYY-MM-DD)?
7. Number + singular after numerals ("2 ház", not "2 házak")?
8. Attachments mentioned if applicable?
9. English translation provided?
10. Tone appropriate for recipient?
11. No em-dashes or en-dashes used as punctuation? Rephrase instead.
